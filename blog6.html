Job Requirements
First of all, it’s a completely wrong concept to think that having skills in just one programming language will guarantee a job. In reality, a job is never just straightforward work.
For example, if you’re looking for a Python-related job, there are usually several additional requirements. First, you need a solid grip on Linux commands. Second, companies often expect expertise in at least one or two frameworks like Django, Django REST Framework, FastAPI, Flask, etc.
Moreover, monolithic architecture is becoming outdated, most companies now work with microservices, so knowledge of microservices architecture is also a common requirement. Then there’s the question of how services communicate with each other, you might need familiarity with tools like Kafka or MQTT.
If you’re a software engineer, strong database skills are also essential both relational databases (like SQL, PostgreSQL) and non-relational databases (like MongoDB).
Companies might also expect server-side knowledge, such as working with cloud services like Azure or AWS.
These are just the very basic requirements. Surprisingly, companies are now also increasingly looking for AI-related skills.
